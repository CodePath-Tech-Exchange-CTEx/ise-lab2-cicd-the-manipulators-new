Usability testing for Challenges

User personas:
Persona : Caleb, 29, A competitive gym member
• Background: Caleb is a gym lover who loves competing with his fellow gym members
• Goals: Effectively compete with his gym lovers by comparing the intensity and amount of 
his completed workout sessions with his friends
• Frustrations: Too easy challenges, Bad metrics for measuring and comparing his 
workouts to his friends

Test tasks:
o Task: Complete the 3-Day Pushup Challenge ? (5 secs)
o Task: Complete the 10 km run Challenge ? (5 secs)

Success criteria:
• Did the participants complete the tasks succesfully within the time frame ?
• How long did each participant spend ?
• Is their average satisfaction rating above 4 out of 5

Test environment:
• The test will be conducted in the college dorm lobby
• The moderator will sit silent next to the participant, Only speaking to tell user tasks 
Test Process for User Persona Caleb

Participant Introduction:
The moderator briefly introduced him self and his app testing. Then the moderator told his user 
his personality: Caleb, a 29 year old competitive gym member who loves competing
Task
• The moderator introduced user to this scenario “You just completed the “3-Days
Challenge: 100 Pushups and Your goal is to ensure the app recognizes you have 
completed the work out app. You clicked on the app and went into challenges”.
• Then the moderator showed the screen:
• The Instruction: The moderator reminded the user about the task and instructed the 
user speak his thought process.
• Moderator Role: Sat slightly behind/beside them. Only speak to provide the next task or 
to prompted interviewee with "What are you thinking right now?" when they went 
silent.

Notes:
The user was initially confused by the paper prototype, not the UI
Then the user recognized the app structure
The user then looked through the screen for 5 seconds and clicked on the button “Complete.”
The user was not confused and clearly demonstrated a sense of familiarity and ease of use with 
the app

Feedback
o How easy/hard was it to complete the task given?
“Task was easy. “
o What confusions were there? Why were they confused?
“No confusions, Task was easy the app was very simple and easy to use.”
o What could be improved?
”I don’t think it needs any improvements”

Result:
During the first 10 seconds, the user was quiet, however I noticed he was confused by the paper 
prototype, not the UI, because after I clarified that was the phone, he navigated through the 
app seamlessly.
During the second task – to complete his second challenge – the user was presented the phone 
with the pop up message, from the previously completed challenge, covering the bottom half 
and the user though to escape it by clicking on a back button that supposed to take him to the 
home screen.
Hyothesis: The user assumed the visible back button would remove the pop up message, which 
is probably why he clicked it, instead, he could have clicked anywhere on the screen to remove 
the pop up message. 
Fix, as the notification message pops up, gray out or make the original Challenge screen unclear, 
to give a sense that tuser could touch anywhere on the screen to cancel the pop up message
Also, Im going to add shadow around each of the challenges and the complete button to make 
button more visible and each challenge attract attention of user
